Six Hacks: How to Reduce Electricity Consumption

Did you know, U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA) stated that the average residential monthly electric bill was calculated as $114.03 in 2015?

Yes, and that is about 9% of every American household’s monthly bills.

It ranks you utility bills – when combined – among the top 5 monthly expenses right after mortgage, insurance, and car payment. Whoa!

But there is always a light at the end of the tunnel, and in this case, we are thankful for these awesome hacks compiled by ChoiceGenie.

How to Air Dry Dishes

Please do not open that freezer a hundred times a day, you are a grown up now!

Sorry, though!

We are being helpful, as every time you open the freezer door, you’re making it consume more energy to keep itself cold.

“Well, I have to get stuff out of it?”, you say?

Try to get them all at once or in groups so you don’t have to let the cool air out of the freezer that many times.

How to is the Dryer Efficient? Check it!

Large articles such as bed sheets have a tendency to get tangled in the dryer.

BUT if you keep a lookout throughout the drying cycle, you can untangle them to dry more quickly.

how to Turn the Main Switch Off

When you are on the way out, turn off your main power! As of course, if you do not mind resetting the TV and alarm clocks.

Most people don’t realize that many devices still consume energy even when they are off.

How to Put the Lights Off

Trust us when we say, NO!

So make a conscious effort to turn off the light as you leave a room. how long you would be away, does not matter.

Not in Use? Turn it OFF

Similar to the theory about the computer, turn off any electrical appliance when you’re not using them.

This covers your TV, Computer, and even gaming consoles.
