ChoiceGenie has come up with solutions to this – threatening – problem, let’s delve into it!

Blackout Solution # 1: Freezer: Check!

Heard of quarter-in-a-cup-of-ice trick?

Well, the name suggests it!

Apply this and you no longer have to worry whether your food is safe to eat when there is a power outage.

Trick # 1: Food is defrosted, if and when the quarter has fallen to the bottom of the cup.

Trick # 2: Food is perfectly edible when the quarter remains either on the top or in the middle of the cup.

Blackout Solution # 2: Create It Yourself Lantern!

Oh, it is pretty interesting

Requirements:

(1) Headlamp or flashlight.

(2) Jug of water

Procedure:

Strap the headlamp or flashlight – if it is feasible – to a jug of water.

Voilà! You have an instant ambient light at your disposal!

Blackout Solution # 3: Glow Sticks: In the Making!

A pretty brilliant trick to light up your way!

Glow sticks come in handy when the lights go out.Use them to line dark hallways in the blackout.

Blackout Solution # 4: DIY a Heater!

Make a flower pot heater!

Hold your horses, whether or not it actually works, is up for the debate.Just to assure you folks, plenty of people have affirmed it on YouTube.A hint: The thermal energy of the candles is better absorbed using Clay/terra-cotta pots. It brilliantly converts into a radiant space heat.

Blackout Solution # 5: Washer or Ice Cooler? You Decide!

Start with filling your washer with ice/snow.

You would notice, it works as a cooler to keep food cold.

The trick is: as the ice melts, the washer will drain.

Don’t you find it a safer option than throwing food out in the snow?

Bonus Point: easily monitored and will not attract animals.
