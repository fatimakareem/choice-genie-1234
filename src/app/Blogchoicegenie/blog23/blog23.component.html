DIY Hacks To Save Energy

Introduction

You think you’re being smart about your energy consumption, yet every month your electric bill never fails to shock you!

Come to think of it, how high it could go in the summer months.

You have to cool off not only you but your apartment too during the scorching mid-July.

Don’t be so shocked, you are among those average Americans who can not help but pay $110 a month on their electric bill.

ChoiceGenie has compiled some pretty awesome hacks for you to try out this summer!

Smart Hack #1: In Mood for Small Meals? Use Small Appliances!

Going to be alone for dinner? How about heating up your food in a toaster oven, which – by the way – can use up to half as much energy as an electric oven.

In the mood for a cup of tea? Bring out the electric kettle and heat the water rather than turning on a stove burner.

When planning on using the stove-top, use the burner closest to the size of your pot.

Smart Hack #2: Use Moisture Sensor Dryer

Activate the moisture sensor feature of your dryer.

As the machine can shut itself off once it senses the clothes are dry.

Smart Hack #3: Please, Turn the Fan Off!

Turn that fan off immediately, when you are done cooling yourself in front of it!

You know it, a fan is only useful when you need the air blowing your way.

So when no intention of going to be anywhere near that fan, simply leaving it on will not achieve the purpose of cooling the entire house down.

Smart Hack #4: Don’t Watch TOO MUCH TV!

If you are planning on saving electricity, it can be a pretty smart habit to turn off the TV when you are not really watching.

Important Fact: a modern flat screen TV can get very hot when it is on for a long time. You got the point!

Smart Hack #5: Garage Door is NOT your Front Door!

ChoiceGenie suggests stop using the garage door as the front door.

If your garage door is electrical powered too then pay heed, door movement uses up electricity!

If you are back home just for a quick stop, it would be one more step towards achieving that resolution of yours – save money on electricity bills – to leave your car in the driveway rather than opening and closing the garage door.

Smart Hack #6: Shut Off the Computer!

One would say, you are simply and intentionally wasting power by leaving the computer at idle state.

ChoiceGenie did warn you!
